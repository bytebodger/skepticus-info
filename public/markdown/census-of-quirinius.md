**The Census of Quirinius (Luke 2)**
The Gospel of Luke attempts to place Jesus' birth during a Roman census while Herod the Great was King of Judea. This creates an impossible timeline.

**The Error:** Herod the Great died in **4 BCE**. Quirinius didn't become governor of Syria (and thus didn't conduct a census) until **6 CE**.

**The Hole:** There's a **10-year gap** between Herod’s death and the census. Furthermore, Roman censuses didn't require citizens to return to their ancestral "hometowns" (like Bethlehem); they were conducted for tax purposes at a person's current place of residence and property.
