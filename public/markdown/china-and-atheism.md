The claim that atheism is responsible for the massive death toll of the Chinese Communist Party (CCP) under Mao Zedong—estimated between **40 and 80 million deaths**—is historically illiterate. While the CCP was, and remains, an officially atheist organization that has aggressively persecuted religious groups, atheism didn't drive the policies that killed tens of millions.

The primary drivers of mass death in 20th-century China were **radical economic experimentation, political paranoia, and a secular cult of personality.**

## 1. The Great Leap Forward (1958–1962): The Deadliest Error

The vast majority of deaths under Mao—estimated at **15 to 45 million**—occurred during the Great Leap Forward. This wasn't a campaign against God; it was a catastrophic failure of centralized economic planning and pseudo-scientific agriculture.

* **Lysenkoism:** The regime adopted the fraudulent agricultural theories of Soviet scientist Trofim Lysenko (such as "close planting"), which led to total crop failure.
* **The Four Pests Campaign:** The state ordered the extermination of sparrows, which caused an ecological collapse and a surge in crop-destroying locust populations.
* **Forced Industrialization:** Farmers were forced to melt their tools in "backyard furnaces" to produce useless pig iron, leaving no one to harvest the remaining food.
* **Causality:** None of these decisions were derived from atheism. They were the result of **Lysenkoist dogma** and a totalitarian refusal to acknowledge reality in the face of ideological goals.

## 2. The Cultural Revolution (1966–1976): Ideological Purity

The Cultural Revolution resulted in an estimated **500,000 to 2 million** deaths. Mao launched it to purge "revisionist" elements and regain control of the party.

* **The Four Olds:** The Red Guards were instructed to destroy "Old Customs, Old Culture, Old Habits, and Old Ideas." This included the destruction of Buddhist temples, Christian churches, and Confucian shrines.
* **The Motivation:** This wasn't a philosophical crusade for atheism; it was a systematic attempt to erase any competing source of authority. For Mao, religion wasn't just "false"; it was a rival power structure.
* **Political Violence:** Most victims were killed because they were labeled "class enemies," "rightists," or "capitalist roaders." These labels were based on political and social standing, not theological belief.

## 3. Where State Atheism Does Bear Direct Blame

Atheism as a state mandate *does* have a direct body count in China, specifically regarding the persecution of religious minorities. However, even here, the primary motivation is **totalitarian control**, not philosophical disagreement.

* **Tibet:** Since the 1950s, the CCP has sought to dismantle Tibetan Buddhism. Thousands of monasteries were destroyed, and monks were executed or imprisoned. This was driven by the need to integrate Tibet into the Chinese state and eliminate the Dalai Lama’s political influence.
* **Current Persecution:** The ongoing detention of Uyghur Muslims in Xinjiang and the suppression of Falun Gong and "underground" Christian churches are contemporary examples. The CCP views any organization with a higher loyalty than the State as a national security threat.

## 4. Maoism as a "Secular Religion"

A key error in the "atheism caused it" argument is the failure to recognize that Maoism replaced traditional religion with a **secular religion**.

Under Mao, the CCP didn't create a society of rationalist freethinkers. It created a society of zealots.

* **The Little Red Book** became a sacred text.
* **Mao Zedong** became an infallible deity.
* **Confession and "Struggle Sessions"** functioned as ritualized, public acts of penance.

The atrocities weren't the result of a "lack of belief." They were the result of a **surplus of belief** in a radical, utopian political dogma that demanded the total subordination of the individual to the Great Helmsman.

## Conclusion

If you want to blame atheism for the deaths in China, you have to ignore the actual reasons people died. People didn't starve during the Great Leap Forward because Mao didn't believe in God; they starved because he believed in Lysenko. People weren't beaten to death during the Cultural Revolution because they were theists; they were beaten because they were perceived as obstacles to Mao’s absolute power.

Atheism was a tool used to strip away competing loyalties, but the engine of the massacres was **totalitarian Maoist ideology**.
