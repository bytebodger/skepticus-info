The best archaeological and demographic estimate for the total population of Egypt during the 13th century BCE (the Ramesside period) is between **3 million and 4.5 million people**.

While figures vary across different scholarly models, the consensus relies on the "carrying capacity" of the Nile Valley and the density of known settlement sites.

Estimates range from 2.1 million (on the low end) to ~4 million on the high end.

**Why these numbers matter**
To understand the scale of the "Moses vs. Pharaoh" events, you have to look at the demographic contradictions between the archaeological record and the biblical narrative:

**The Urban Cap:** The capital at the time, **Pi-Ramesses**, likely housed between **100,000 and 250,000 people**. It was one of the largest cities in the ancient world, but it couldn't have supported the millions described in literalist religious texts.

**The Carrying Capacity:** The amount of arable land in the Nile Valley during the New Kingdom was roughly 17,000–20,000 square kilometers. A population of 3.5 million results in a density of about **175–200 people per $km^2$**. Any number significantly higher than 5 million would've led to systemic famine, as the agricultural tech of the Bronze Age couldn't produce enough surplus.

**The "Holes" in the Biblical Figure:** The Book of Exodus (12:37) claims **600,000 men** left Egypt. When you include women, children, and elderly, that's a total group of **2 to 3 million people**.

If the total Egyptian population was 4 million, the Exodus would represent **50–75% of the entire country's population** leaving at once.

There's zero archaeological evidence of a sudden 75% demographic collapse or the logistical footprint such a massive migration would leave in the Sinai Peninsula.

**The Bottom Line**
If you're looking for the most scientifically grounded number, stick to **4 million**. Any estimate pushing toward 10 million or higher ignores the physical reality of ancient agricultural limits. If you're trying to reconcile this with the biblical account, the 600,000 figure is likely a symbolic number or a mistranslation of the word 'eleph (which can mean "clan" or "unit" rather than "thousand").
